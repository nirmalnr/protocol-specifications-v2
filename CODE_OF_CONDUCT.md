# Code of Conduct

**Scope:** All participation spaces across Beckn Core Working Group governed repositories — `protocol-specifications-v2`, `schemas`, `DEG`, and derivative repositories — including GitHub Discussions, Issues, Pull Requests, working group meetings, and any other collaboration channel associated with these repositories.

---

## Purpose

Beckn Protocol is critical infrastructure for a global, decentralized ecosystem. The people who maintain it and contribute to it come from diverse backgrounds, domains, and regions. The standards of conduct in this repository reflect the standards appropriate for infrastructure of this significance: direct, evidence-based, respectful, and process-bound.

This Code of Conduct does not exist to make participation comfortable for everyone at all times. It exists to ensure that the process through which the protocol evolves is trustworthy — that decisions are made on technical merit, that the process is followed by everyone regardless of seniority or affiliation, and that the working group can do its work without pressure, harassment, or bad-faith argumentation.

---

## Expected behavior

All contributors, reviewers, and working group members are expected to:

- Communicate in formal, precise, and constructive terms.
- Ground all technical claims in evidence — reproducible test cases, conformance findings, or documented ecosystem impact.
- Accept working group decisions, review outcomes, and process timelines, even when those outcomes are unfavorable.
- Follow the RFC lifecycle and governance model as documented in [GOVERNANCE.md](./GOVERNANCE.md) without exception.
- Disclose relevant affiliations, conflicts of interest, or downstream dependencies when participating in reviews or votes that affect those interests.
- Treat every other participant's time as a finite and valuable resource.

---

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors will result in moderation action:

- Personal attacks, harassment, intimidation, or abusive language directed at any participant.
- Repeated argumentation on a decision that has been documented and finalized by the working group.
- Deliberately bypassing the RFC lifecycle — submitting PRs without approved RFCs, targeting `main` directly, or committing artifacts outside the documented process.
- Pressuring maintainers, reviewers, or working group members to force a merge, accelerate a timeline, or override a review decision.
- Misrepresenting consensus, approval status, or review outcomes in any forum.
- Submitting non-conformant Pull Requests repeatedly after receiving documented notice of violations.
- Using a contributor's organizational affiliation, ecosystem position, or downstream adoption volume as an argument for expediting or overriding the process.

On the last point: the governance model is designed to be resistant to influence from any single actor regardless of their size or significance in the ecosystem. A large network operator does NOT receive a faster review than an independent contributor. The protocol does NOT change because a significant implementer finds a constraint inconvenient.

---

## Process bypass is a conduct violation

Bypassing the RFC lifecycle is NOT a technical infraction that results in a PR being closed and resubmitted. It is a conduct violation. The RFC process exists to protect the ecosystem from the consequences of undiscussed, unconsidered, or rushed protocol changes. Bypassing it is an assertion that one's own judgment supersedes the working group's authority — which it does not.

Contributors who submit PRs without completed Proposal Discussions, who target `main` directly, or who repeatedly ignore the RFC gate after documented notice are treated under this Code of Conduct, not merely under technical review policy.

---

## Enforcement and access restrictions

Moderation follows an escalating ladder. All actions are documented in the associated Issue, PR, or Discussion thread and reported to the Working Group Administrator.

| Stage | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Notice** | First documented violation | PR, Issue, or Discussion closed with documented reason. Contributor notified in writing. |
| **Suspension** | Repeated violations after notice | PR submission privileges suspended. |
| **Restriction** | Continued violations after suspension | Issue submission and Discussion participation restricted. |
| **Removal** | Severe, persistent, or bad-faith violations | Contributor removed from the organization across all governed repositories. |

Maintainers do NOT issue warnings informally or verbally. Every enforcement action is documented. The documentation serves as the record for any reinstatement request.

---

## Reinstatement

A contributor who has been removed and wishes to be reinstated MUST submit a formal written request to the Working Group Administrator. The request MUST:

- Identify the specific violations that led to removal.
- State the root cause of those violations.
- Describe what remediation the contributor has taken.

The Core Working Group reviews reinstatement requests at its discretion. It reserves the right to accept or deny any request without obligation to provide justification. Reinstatement is NOT guaranteed by the passage of time alone.

---

## Applicability

This Code of Conduct applies to all participation in spaces associated with Beckn Core Working Group governed repositories, including but not limited to: GitHub Discussions, Issues, Pull Requests, working group meetings, and any other collaboration channel designated by the working group.

It applies equally to contributors, reviewers, working group members, and Core Committers. No participant is exempt.
